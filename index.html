Your prompt

I'm part of a small working group at Indiana's AI Roadshow Community Workshop. Our group's shared theme is: [ X ]. Here's what already exists in our community around this: [ Y ]. Here's what's missing or falling short: [ Z ]. A few things about our region: our industry mix is [ e.g., manufacturing-heavy, mixed small retail, healthcare-anchored ], our employer landscape is [ e.g., no single large employer / one dominant employer / several mid-size firms ], and trusted partners we could tap are [ e.g., a community college, workforce board, a high-profile employer willing to sponsor ]. Help us sketch 2–3 concrete, doable micro-learning or engagement opportunities we could realistically launch in the next 60–90 days — tailored to our region's specific shape, not a generic one-size-fits-all program.

A few ideas to spark your thinking

Not a menu to pick from — just ideas to react to, since a blank page is harder than a starting point.

AI Ambassador

A few employees already comfortable with AI informally coach their peers.

Digital Navigator

A dedicated person (often workforce-board- or library-funded) does one-on-one help.

See a real example →

Higher-ed partnership

Team up with a local community college or university for short AI-literacy sessions.

Anchor-employer cohort

A high-profile local employer sponsors space and credibility for a peer cohort.

Adopt, don't build

Localize an existing free curriculum — like the U.S. Chamber's "Small Business B(AI)sics" — instead of starting from scratch.

See a real example →

Peer use-case swap

A recurring session where businesses that already tried something share what worked.
